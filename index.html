Bengaluru

The city that inspired me.

Here are some interesting facts about Bengaluru

    Founded by Kempe Gowda in 1537
  • It is a city of lakes.
  • Happy weather and higher altitude.
  • Focal point of IT industries and home for many start-ups in India.
  • Amazing south Indian cuisine.
  • Highest traffic density.
  • Center for Aerospace.
  • Hub for Educational institutions.
  • Has highest number of pubs in India.
  • Shivanasamudram Dam is Asia’s first hydroelectric power station.
  • The Mughals once invaded Bengaluru.
  • It has an excellent public transport system.
  • It has the most two-wheelers/mopeds.
  • Deadly explosive used in world war 1&2 was Bangalore Torpedo.
  • It has the oldest army regiments in India.
  • Home to more than 1000 distinct species of flora and fauna.

The reputation of the IT hub and the 'Silicon Valley' of India comes to Bangalore.Beyond the blissful ambiance of the city, Bengaluru has an interesting history, has lots to do and is also the gateway of many of the tourist attractions of the city.The city is dotted with cafes, bars, pubs, eateries, etc. Bangalore nightlife is one of the best in India, with the after dark hours offering the best time for visitors. The entertainment options in the city, don’t stop here. Fun waterparks, amusement parks, shopping hubs keep you on your toes. In contrast and co-existing peacefully within the city are forts, temples and architectural wonders.The people in the city are warm, hospitable and friendly, helping you create cherished memories of a Bangalore holiday.
